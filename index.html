Why He's Doing It

A Walk With Purpose

Jeff Lancaster is walking the full length of Britain — roughly 1,000 miles from the granite cliffs of Land's End to the windswept tip of John O'Groats — in support of Brain Tumour Research.

Brain tumours are the biggest cancer killer of children and adults under 40 in the UK, yet receive just 1% of national cancer research funding. Jeff is putting one foot in front of the other to help change that.

Follow along on Strava as Jeff logs every mile, and on Instagram @jefflancaster1000 for daily dispatches from the road.

The Cause

Why Brain Tumours?

Brain tumours are one of the most underfunded and deadly cancers in the UK. Despite being the leading cancer killer of under-40s, research funding has historically been woefully low.

The Charity

Brain Tumour Research

Brain Tumour Research is a leading UK charity funding world-class research at dedicated centres. Their mission is to find a cure for all types of brain tumours.

They campaign for greater investment, fight for better treatment options, and support the thousands of patients and families living with this devastating disease.
